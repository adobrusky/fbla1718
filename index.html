APPLYING

Although there are many different ways for you to begin your job search, we suggest you first take an assessment to help identify your job skills. Next, our experts at JOBTIPS have determined that it is better to tailor your resume for each individual job description, so you need to find jobs first. Here is a list of the best places to find available jobs.


  • Attend job fairs
  • Network: talk to others about your job search
  • Browse online job banks like our JOBTIPS job finder
  • Search through newspaper ads
  • Research and contact prospective employers
  • Set up interviews with prospective employers
  • Join job organizations/clubs in your area
  • Attend training sessions or build skills desired by prospective employers

    WRITING A RESUME & COVER LETTER

    An excellent resume has the power to open doors. Your resume describes your qualifications and what makes you unique. To stand out among other applicants, you need a resume that markets your strengths and match for the job. You might start with a generic resume but it needs to be individually tailored for each job!

    A great resume:

    • Grabs the attention of employers and recruiters
    • Has organized Information
    • Is readable/neat
    • Has grammar and spelling perfection
    • Clearly presents your contact information
    • Sells your strongest skills and accomplishments
    • Highlights relevant education/training
    • List Awards and Special accomplishments
    • Shows how you’re a match for a position or project
    • Contains meaningful key words emphasizing your skills
    • Shows you are available, flexible and familiar with the company
    • And most importantly, gets you a job interview!

    Elements of a Resume:

    • Top portion of resume (first third to half)
    • Header
      • Name you go by that stands out on page
      • Contact information address, phone, email
    • Headline and Summary
      • What you’re looking for in a career with this company
      • How you’re qualified
      • Announces your job target and sums up why you’re a good candidate
    • Special Skills
      • List relevant skills peculiar to the job
      • Skills you possess that are unique and you consider valuable
    • Work Experience or Professional Experience or Employment History
      • List employment history, latest first
      • Include volunteer experience
    • Education/Continuing Education and Professional Development
      • List education most recent first
      • Include education in progress or partially complete if relevant
    • Other Information
      • Professional memberships
      • Awards that support your candidacy

    INTERVIEWS

    The interview is the second most important part of applying for a job besides the actual application. Here are some tips on having a successful interview:

    • Arrive 10-15 minutes early to the interview
    • Let the interviewer start the dialogue
      • Be confident but don’t appear too eager
      • Don’t interrupt
      • Don’t be afraid to ask for clarification
    • Practice in front of the mirror
    • Be prepared
      • Know facts/news about the company
      • Find out about the person conducting the interview
      • Re-read the job description
    • Make a list of questions
      • Clarify anything you are unclear about in the job description
      • What benefits are available
      • Opportunities for development and promotion
    • Maintain eye contact
      • Conveys confidence and interest
      • Helps the interviewer establish a relationship
    • Dress professionally
      • Many interviews require business attire
      • Some interviews may require special attire
      • Grooming and hygiene are always important
    • Positive Attitude
      • Try to state your answers in a positive way
      • Do not degrade any past co-worker or company
